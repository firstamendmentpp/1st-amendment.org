Privacy Policy

Effective Date: September 21, 2025

1st-Amendment.org (“we,” “us,” or “our”) is committed to protecting your privacy while providing access to our website. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your choices regarding that information.

1. Information We Collect

We collect technical and usage information to provide and improve our services:

  • IP addresses: Collected automatically by Cloudflare for security, network performance, and traffic management.
  • Device and browser information: Including browser type, screen size, operating system, and device type.
  • Usage analytics: Tracked via Google Analytics to understand page visits, clicks, session duration, and website performance.
  • Log data: Server logs maintained by Cloudflare include information about your requests to our site.

Note: We do not collect personal information such as names, email addresses, phone numbers, payment details, or physical addresses unless voluntarily provided.

2. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We do not use cookies for personal tracking.

  • Google Analytics and Cloudflare may use temporary cookies for session analytics and security.
  • Cookies help us analyze site performance and detect malicious activity.
  • You can configure your browser to block cookies, though this may slightly affect functionality.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To ensure website security and prevent attacks.
  • To monitor and improve website performance.
  • To analyze general usage patterns for future updates.

4. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

We may share non-identifiable data with third-party services:

  • Cloudflare: Provides CDN, security, and traffic optimization services.
  • Google Analytics: Collects anonymized usage statistics to help us understand how users interact with the site.

We do not sell, rent, or otherwise share personal information with advertisers or other third parties.

5. Data Protection Measures

  • Cloudflare security features protect the site from attacks and unauthorized access.
  • Analytics data is stored in a secure cloud environment.
  • Regular monitoring for unusual activity ensures security.

6. Your Rights and Choices

  • Opt out of analytics tracking by disabling cookies in your browser.
  • Contact us at firstamendmentpp@gmail.com for questions or requests.

7. Updates to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updates will be posted directly on this page with a revised “Effective Date.” Significant changes may be announced via the website or social channels.

8. Contact Information

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or our practices, please contact:
